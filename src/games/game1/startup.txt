*title The Forked Path
*author Anonymous

You wake at a fork in a dark wood. The path splits in two: one leads east toward a faint glow, the other west toward the smell of pine and woodsmoke.

What do you do?

*choice
  #Walk east toward the glow.
    You step toward the light. As you draw closer, you see a small lantern hanging from a low branch, and beside it, a folded note.
    *page_break
    The note reads: "Whoever you are, you are not lost. Take the lantern. Keep going."
    *page_break
    You take the lantern and walk on. The path widens, and so does the world.
    *finish
  #Walk west toward the smoke.
    The smell grows stronger. Soon you find a small clearing with a campfire and a kettle on a tripod. A stranger sits across from the fire and looks up, unsurprised.
    *page_break
    "There you are," they say. "Sit. The tea is almost ready."
    *page_break
    You sit. The fire is warm. For the first time tonight, you feel found.
    *finish
  #Stay at the fork and wait for morning.
    You sit down with your back against a tree and close your eyes. The wood is quiet. You are tired enough that the choosing can wait.
    *page_break
    When you wake, sunlight is on your face and a robin is shouting in a tree. The paths look smaller in the day. You pick one without much ceremony, and walk.
    *finish
