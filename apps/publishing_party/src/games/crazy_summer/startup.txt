*label page1
Every summer, you begin your summer break with a visit to your grandparents’ house. You just finished 6th grade, your first year of middle school, and you are excited to share your first-year experience with them.[n/]
Your parents drive you to your grandparents’ house in their blue car. When you arrive, your grandparents are gone. They left you a note that says, “We had to fill up the car with gas. We’ll be right back.”[n/]
You think, Maybe I could surprise them by cleaning their garage.[n/]
Your parents think that is a good idea, too, and say, “Yeah! Sure!”[n/]
You go into the garage. There are so many boxes full of holiday decorations. It is so dark that you can hardly see anything.[n/]
After what seems like a long time, you hear the honk of your grandparents’ car. You pull the garage lever, expecting it to open the door so you can see your grandparents waiting for you.[n/]
Instead, the garage door never opens.[n/]
You are confused. You flick the lever again.[n/]
All of a sudden, the ground begins to shake. A large trap door that you never noticed creaks open, and you fall through at top speed.[n/]
You hit the ground.[n/]
It is too high up for you to get out. You call out for help, but no one answers.[n/]
Oh no, where am I? you think to yourself. How will I get back to my grandparents’ house?[n/]
Suddenly, three portals appear right in front of you.[n/]
